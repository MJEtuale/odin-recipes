Sapasui (chop suey) Recipe

Description

Made from vermicelli, your choice vegetables or not and your favourite meat. This will definitely be a crowd pleaser and an easy to make dish that can be made for 2 people, a family, or your village.

This dish has been inspired by the asain influence in the South Pacific and thus the Island nations have adapted it to their liking with what they had from the land and sea.

We hope you enjoy your Sapasui today and make it an addition to your favourite recipes

Ingredients

  • 3 packs of Vermicelli
  • 1 x Onion
  • 1 x Garlic
  • thumb-sized piece of Ginger
  • 1 bag of frozen Long beans
  • 2 x Capsicums
  • 2kg of Lamb meat
  • 500g of Beef mince
  • Olive or Rice oil
  • 1 x bowl of water

Steps:

  1. Finely chop up all your ingredients except for the capsicum, long beans and the meat.
  2. Cut your capsicums into lpong strips.
  3. Roughly chop your lamb meat to your preferred size.
  4. Place your vermicelli in a bowl and fill it so that the water covers all the vermicelli.
  5. Place your meat in a pot and fill with water so that it covers the meat and boil till the water is gone and onluy the fat is at the bottom.
  6. Add some oil and your onion, garlic and ginger.
  7. Throw in your vegetables.
  8. Put some soy sauce into the mix till you are happy.
  9. Take a scissors and cut up the vermicelli in the water till they are at the length you prefer then add that to the pot.
  10. Let it boil for 15min, taste if it is seasoned enough. Add seasoning if you wish.
  11. Enjoy your Sapasui, with some rice or taro.
